Product & Features

What exactly is VetScan?

VetScan is an AI-powered triage companion for new dog parents. You describe a symptom, the AI asks clarifying questions like a vet would, and gives you a clear urgency score: 🟢 watch and wait, 🟡 see a vet within 24-48 hours, or 🔴 emergency now.

It's designed for that anxious moment between "something seems off" and "should I rush to the ER?" — typically the moment first-time dog parents face frequently in the first 24 months.

Is VetScan a replacement for my vet?

No. We say this loudly because it's true. VetScan is a smart first step that helps you decide if and when to call your vet — not a substitute for professional veterinary care.

Your vet does things VetScan never will: physical exams, blood work, X-rays, surgery, prescriptions. We complement that, we don't replace it.

What kinds of symptoms does it cover?

VetScan covers the most common symptoms that drive new dog parents to worry:

  • Vomiting, diarrhea, appetite loss
  • Limping, lameness, mobility issues
  • Skin issues — itching, rashes, lumps
  • Eye and ear concerns
  • Behavior changes — lethargy, restlessness
  • Eating something they shouldn't have
  • Breathing changes
  • Urinary issues

For uncommon symptoms or rare presentations, VetScan errs on the side of "see a vet" rather than guessing.

Can I upload photos?

Yes. For visible issues — skin conditions, eye irritation, wound assessment, posture concerns — you can snap a photo and the AI will use it as part of the triage. Photos help with assessment but never replace a hands-on vet exam.

How long does a check take?

About 60 seconds for most checks. Describe what's happening, answer 3-5 follow-ups, get your urgency score and explanation. The whole experience is built for a stressed dog parent at midnight, not for a leisurely browse.

Does VetScan track my dog's health over time?

Yes. Every check you run becomes part of your dog's health history in the app. Next time something happens, VetScan already knows context — breed, age, past symptoms, previous vet visits — to give more accurate guidance.

You can export this history to share with your vet, or delete it any time. It's your data.

AI & Accuracy

How accurate is the AI?

For common, well-documented conditions, our internal testing shows VetScan's triage matches recommendations a licensed veterinarian would give in the same scenario. We continuously test against new cases reviewed by practicing vets.

For unusual or atypical presentations, VetScan defaults to "see a vet" rather than guessing. We'd rather send you to your vet unnecessarily once than miss something serious.

Can the AI be wrong?

Yes. AI makes mistakes. Always trust your gut: if something feels off about your dog — even if VetScan says "watch and wait" — go to the vet. We will never get offended.

This is also why we cap urgency at "see a vet within 24-48 hours" for most ambiguous cases. We err toward more vet visits, not fewer.

How was the AI trained?

VetScan combines large language models (similar to ChatGPT, but specialized) with a structured triage protocol developed with practicing small-animal veterinarians.

The protocol encodes the standard decision trees vets use during phone triage and intake assessments. The language model lets us interpret your natural-language descriptions and ask the right follow-up questions.

We're transparent about this: we're not making mystical "AI vet" claims. We're encoding existing clinical knowledge into an accessible interface.

What if my dog has a rare condition?

For rare or unusual presentations, VetScan defaults to recommending a vet visit. We don't try to diagnose rare conditions — that's a job for veterinary specialists with diagnostic equipment.

If you've already been diagnosed with a chronic condition (e.g., kidney disease, diabetes, allergies), you can add it to your dog's profile. The AI will factor that in for future symptom checks.

Will it work for my breed?

VetScan supports all dog breeds. The AI considers breed-specific risk factors — for example, French Bulldogs are flagged differently for breathing issues than Greyhounds, and large breed puppies get specific guidance about joint development.

For mixed breeds, you can specify primary breed mix or "mixed/unknown" — the AI handles both.

Veterinary Relationship

Will my vet be okay with me using this?

Most vets we've spoken with welcome it. Their biggest frustration isn't dog parents using AI — it's dog parents using Google. VetScan helps you arrive at appointments more informed and less panicked, which makes the visit easier for everyone.

It also helps you avoid unnecessary emergency visits, freeing up your vet's time and your wallet for the cases that actually need them.

Can I share VetScan reports with my vet?

Yes. You can export any check or your dog's full health history as a PDF or shareable link. Many users share their VetScan reports with the vet at the start of an appointment to save time.

What if my vet disagrees with VetScan's assessment?

Always go with your vet. They've physically examined your dog, run tests, and have years of clinical training. VetScan operates on description and pattern matching — your vet operates on direct evidence.

If you find a recurring discrepancy, please email us at hello@vetscan.app with details (with your vet's permission). It helps us improve.

Are veterinarians involved in building VetScan?

Yes. We work with practicing small-animal veterinarians who review our triage protocols, audit AI outputs, and advise on clinical accuracy. Their names and credentials will be published once we launch publicly.

If you're a practicing vet interested in collaborating, email hello@vetscan.app — we'd love to talk.

Privacy & Data

Is my data private?

Yes. We never sell your dog's data to anyone — pet food companies, insurance providers, advertisers, no exceptions. You can export everything or delete your account at any time.

For details, see our Privacy Policy.

Do you train AI on my data?

Only with your explicit consent, and only in anonymized form. You can opt out at any time in settings. If you opt out, your past data will not be used for training, and your future data won't either.

Can I delete my account?

Yes, anytime. Settings → Account → Delete account. We delete all your data within 30 days. If you have specific GDPR or CCPA deletion requests, email privacy@vetscan.app.

Is VetScan GDPR / CCPA compliant?

Yes. We support all GDPR rights for EU/UK users (access, deletion, portability, objection) and CCPA rights for California residents (know, delete, opt-out). See our Privacy Policy for details on how to exercise these rights.

Technical

What devices does VetScan work on?

iOS and Android at launch. Web app comes later (web-first triage works in browser, but the full mobile experience is the priority).

  • iOS: 16.0 or later
  • Android: 12.0 or later
Does it work offline?

Some features work offline: emergency symptom flagging, your saved reports and dog profile, pre-cached emergency vet contacts. Full AI-powered triage requires an internet connection.

How fast is it?

Each check takes about 60 seconds end-to-end. The AI thinking step itself takes 3-5 seconds. The rest is you describing the issue and answering follow-ups.
